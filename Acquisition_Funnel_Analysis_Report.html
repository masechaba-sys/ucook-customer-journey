Pain Points

🚨 Red Flags

Critical issues requiring immediate investigation and remediation.

1. Subscription Plans/Menu Page: 98.4% Abandonment Critical

103,387 users reach the Plans Page or Meal Kit Menu Page — but only 1,697 proceed to confirm a subscription. This is the single biggest revenue leak across both funnels. Something about this page is causing almost all users to leave: pricing friction, overwhelming choice, a lack of trust signals, or an unclear value proposition.

103,387 entered 101,690 abandoned Only 1,697 continued 98.4% drop-off

2. On Demand Product Page: 90% Abandonment Critical

Of the 29,413 users who reach the Frozen Product Page, only 2,916 add to cart. This 90.1% abandonment rate suggests the product page is not closing the intent that was generated at the Home Page. Poor product presentation, price anchoring, limited product variety, or a confusing UX are likely contributors.

90.1% abandonment 9.9% proceed to cart 26,497 lost here

3. Mobile Underperforms Desktop by 2.5× Across the Board Critical

Mobile accounts for 78.9% of all traffic yet converts at just 0.52% end-to-end, versus 1.33% on desktop. The gap is present at every funnel step — product page engagement (8.8% vs 14.0%), checkout completion (38.8% vs 56.5%), and overall purchase rate. With mobile being the dominant channel, every percentage point improvement here has outsized revenue impact.

0.52% mobile e2e 1.33% desktop e2e 2.5× gap 78.9% of traffic is mobile

4. Google Ads Traffic Underperforms in the Subscription Funnel Concern

Google Ads is the second largest traffic source (61,252 visitors) but converts to subscription purchase at only 0.09% — far below (not set) traffic at 0.49%. Critically, it has the lowest Plans→Confirm conversion at 1.30%, and even its Home→Plans step is the weakest among sources (49.1%). Given this is paid traffic with a clear cost-per-click, the ROI on Google Ads for the subscription product needs immediate review.

0.09% sub conversion Paid traffic 49.1% reach Plans page vs 68.5% for (not set)

5. Manual Traffic Has Weak Cart Completion in On Demand Concern

Manual / organic traffic converts from cart to purchase at only 34.5% in the On Demand funnel — 22 percentage points below (not set) users (56.3%). Since organic traffic represents the largest source (80,421 visitors), improving cart completion for this segment would have meaningful revenue impact.

34.5% cart→purchase (manual) 56.3% cart→purchase (not set)

6. Shopping Free Listings: Zero Conversions Monitor

36 users arrived via Shopping Free Listings over 90 days. Zero proceeded past the Home Page in the On Demand funnel, and only 7 reached the Plans page in the Subscription funnel (none converted). While the volume is currently tiny, this channel is yielding no value and may indicate a product feed mismatch or incorrect landing page routing.

0 On Demand purchases 0 Subscription purchases 36 users, 90 days

Wins

✅ What's Working

Validated strengths and signals that should be amplified and replicated across weaker segments.

🏠  55% of All Visitors Navigate to Plans/Menu (Strong Top-of-Funnel Intent) Win

Over half of all 187,405 visitors proceed from the Home Page to the Plans or Meal Kit Menu page — demonstrating that UCOOK's homepage is effectively generating subscription consideration. The problem is not attracting interested users; it's converting them once they reach the plans page. The intent is there; the page needs to close it.

💳  76.2% of Subscription Add-to-Cart Users Complete Purchase Win

Once a user adds a subscription to cart (412 users), 76.2% follow through to purchase (314 users). This is a strong signal that checkout friction is relatively low and that users who cross the Confirm Subscription step are highly committed. The bottleneck is upstream — getting more users to Confirm.

📈  Google Ads Has the Strongest Final Purchase Conversion in Subscription (82.6%) Win

Among users who add a subscription to cart, Google Ads converts 82.6% to purchase — the highest of any source. This means the Google Ads audience, once deeply engaged in the funnel, is highly motivated to buy. The challenge is the earlier funnel steps. If Google Ads landing and plans-page experience can be improved, this closing strength could deliver significantly more revenue.

🎯  (Not Set) / Returning Users Convert 3× Better at Every Step Win

(Not set) users — likely brand-familiar, returning, or direct visitors — convert at 3.52% on the Plans→Confirm step vs 0.49% for Manual and 1.30% for Google Ads. Their end-to-end subscription conversion of 0.49% is nearly 5× the Manual baseline (0.04%). This is the benchmark: understanding what makes these users convert and replicating the experience for cold traffic is the key strategic insight from this data.

🖥  Desktop Checkout Is Strong: 56.5% Cart-to-Purchase in On Demand Win

Desktop users complete the On Demand cart-to-purchase step at 56.5% — a healthy checkout completion rate. Paired with a 14.0% product-page-to-cart rate (vs 8.8% mobile), desktop shows the full funnel can work well. The challenge is that desktop is only 20% of traffic, and the mobile experience must be brought up to match this standard.

🔁  Brand Familiarity Dramatically Improves Every Metric Win

Across both funnels, the (not set) cohort — representing users with prior exposure to UCOOK — outperforms cold acquisition traffic at every single funnel step. In the Subscription funnel they also have the highest Home→Plans progression at 68.5%. Investing in retention, remarketing, and brand recall for acquisition traffic is likely to yield stronger funnel-wide improvements than pure top-of-funnel volume.

Next Steps

Priority Recommendations

Ranked by potential impact based on funnel volume and drop-off severity.

1

Redesign the Plans / Meal Kit Menu Page Experience

Audit the Plans page for friction: pricing clarity, plan comparison layout, social proof, and CTA prominence. Consider A/B testing a simplified plan selection flow with fewer choices, stronger benefit messaging, and mobile-first layout. The 98.4% drop-off from 103k users makes this the highest-return fix on the site.

🚨 Highest Impact · Subscription
2

Optimise the Mobile Funnel End-to-End

Run a full mobile UX audit across the product page (tap targets, image quality, product copy), cart experience, and checkout flow. The 2.5× gap between mobile and desktop suggests systemic friction — not isolated bugs. Prioritise mobile checkout form usability, payment method options, and page load speed.

⚠️ High Impact · Both Funnels
3

Improve the Frozen Product Page to Lift Add-to-Cart Rate

The 90% abandonment on the product page is severe. Review product imagery quality, description copy, pricing presentation, and the prominence of the add-to-cart CTA. Consider introducing social proof elements (reviews, ratings, purchase counts) and clearer delivery / freshness guarantees directly on product cards.

⚠️ High Impact · On Demand
4

Review Google Ads Landing Page Alignment for Subscription

Google Ads traffic is not converting in the subscription funnel (0.09%) despite being a paid, intent-driven channel. Review whether ads are landing on the correct subscription pages, whether ad copy matches the landing page offer, and whether targeting is reaching new vs. returning user segments appropriately. Given Google Ads' strong close rate (82.6%) once engaged, fixing the top-of-funnel alignment could significantly improve paid ROAS.

📊 Medium Impact · Subscription
5

Investigate and Attribute "(Not Set)" Traffic

The (not set) cohort converts at 3–5× the rate of attributed sources. Understanding who these users are — likely direct traffic, returning customers, or referrals with broken UTM tagging — is critical. Improving UTM attribution across all campaigns and channels will allow UCOOK to understand which efforts are actually driving high-value converters and invest accordingly.

🔍 Strategic · Both Funnels
6

Review or Pause Shopping Free Listings

36 users, 0 conversions in 90 days. While the volume is low, the 0% conversion rate warrants a review of the product feed, landing page routing, and whether the channel is reaching the right audience. Either optimise the feed and landing experience or de-prioritise the channel.

📋 Low Priority · Quick Win
