1. Executive Summary

This report analyses 21,859 UCOOK support tickets submitted between April 2025 and March 2026, spanning 12 months across Email, WhatsApp, Web, and social channels. A further 4,242 tickets from Skoon — a separate brand sharing the Zendesk instance — have been excluded from all metrics.

The analysis identifies eight recurring customer contact themes, maps them to the five pain-point clusters defined in the Customer Journey Workshop (Session 1), and tracks how each cluster has evolved month-by-month.

Key Findings

Address & Delivery dominates

The single largest pain point, consistently 33–53% of monthly ticket volume across all 12 months.

Sep 2025 subscription spike

44.1% of that month's tickets were pause/cancel related — 1,068 pause_account tickets vs a normal baseline of 25–70/month.

Cancellations trending up +170%

cancelled_during_billing grew from ~50/month mid-2025 to 135–146/month in Q1 2026.

Feb 2026 login incident

Simultaneous spike in Technical Issues (7.8%) and Onboarding & Account (8.6%) — platform auth/reactivation issue.

Compliments = 38.8% of all tickets

Strong positive baseline. January 2026 was the highest compliment month at 51.4%.

Product Quality H2 dip & recovery

Quality reports fell from 3.2% (Apr '25) to near 0% by Dec '25, then recovered in early 2026 — worth validating with procurement.

5. Notable Anomalies & Incidents

⚠ 5.1 · September 2025 — Subscription Management Spike

1,068 pause_account tickets in a single month. Subscription-related tickets surged to 44.1% of all contact volume (vs a 12-month average of ~9%). Driven almost entirely by customer_service__pause_account (1,068 tickets vs a normal range of 25–70/month). The ar_suggest_true tag — indicating auto-responder activation — appeared on 1,080 tickets in the same month, suggesting this may have been triggered by an automated system event or a billing-related communication that prompted mass pauses.

Recommended action: Product and engineering should review the September 2025 billing cycle for system changes, payment failures, or customer comms that could have triggered this. If a root cause is identified, it represents a recoverable churn risk.

⚠ 5.2 · February 2026 — Login & Technical Issues Spike

Tech Issues jump to 7.8% and Onboarding to 8.6%. Unusual co-occurrence of technical (tech: 179, tech__login: 161) and onboarding/account issues (sign-up_assistance: 48). Suggests a platform authentication or reactivation issue affecting both new sign-ups and returning customers. Numbers returned to baseline in March 2026.

Recommended action: Engineering to review deployment and incident logs for January–February 2026 for auth-related regressions. Review customer-facing reactivation messaging for clarity.

⚠ 5.3 · Cancellations Trending Upward (+170% since May 2025)

The customer_service__cancelled_during_billing tag grew steadily from 44 tickets/month in May 2025 to 146/month in February 2026 — the most consistent upward trend across the 12-month dataset. The order_cancellation tag shows a similar but less pronounced trend. This may indicate growing friction in the billing or subscription renewal experience.

Recommended action: Cohort analysis of customers who cancelled during billing to identify whether they had prior delivery, quality, or payment issues. Make the pause flow more prominent as a softer alternative to cancellation.

⚠ 5.4 · December 2025 — Operations Spike

Operations & Fulfilment tickets rose to 8.3% in December 2025 (vs 12-month average of 5.5%), driven by missing_ingredient and missing_meal reports. Consistent with festive-season supply chain pressure and increased order volumes. A similar pattern may recur in December 2026.

6. Cluster Deep Dives

6.1 · Address & Delivery Logistics

High Priority · 38.2%

8,340 tickets over 12 months · UCOOK's highest-volume contact driver

Peaks in April–May 2025 (52.6% and 45.2%) and dips during winter, likely reflecting lower order volumes. Covers address changes, delivery day changes, non-delivery events, driver complaints, and box return queries.

Key sub-themes: delivery_status queries (3,227), order_address_change (3,116), customer_service__address_change_request (468), delivery_day_change_request (214).

Customer Voice

Web"Hello. I need to change the delivery address of my order for Monday 19 May please."
Email"Good morning — we are away on Monday and only back Tuesday at 3pm. Would it be possible to have our delivery delayed until then? The address is 23 Chelsea Avenue in Vredehoek."
Web"Can my delivery be scheduled for Tuesday? No one is home tomorrow."

Trend Insight

While consistently high, this cluster's share is actually declining as a proportion of total tickets (52.6% in April 2025 → 39.9% in March 2026), suggesting either delivery self-service improvements are reducing contact, or other clusters are growing proportionally.

Opportunities

  • Enable address and delivery-day changes directly in the app or WhatsApp flow without requiring CS contact.
  • Send proactive delivery-day reminders with a self-service change link, reducing inbound queries.
  • Build a driver-tracking link into the dispatch confirmation SMS/email.

6.2 · Subscription Management

High Priority · 14.0%

3,051 tickets · extreme variance, driven by the September 2025 pause event

Excluding the September 2025 anomaly (§5.1), the monthly range is 7–14%. Covers pause requests, cancellations, skip requests, and general subscription service queries. The upward trend in cancellations (§5.3) is the key signal here.

Key sub-themes: customer_service__pause_account (1,545 total · 1,068 in Sep alone), cancelled_during_billing (998), order_cancellation (614), how_to_pause_subscription (256).

Customer Voice

API"Paused. Reason: I wasn't happy with my last order. Customer has lifetime orders of 20 and lifetime subscription revenue of R9,298."
Web"I do not want to sign up for a contract. I just want to order now and then."
Web"Hi, I never confirmed an order for next week. There was nothing in my cart. Please can you credit my account. I don't eat ostrich."

Trend Insight

Outside of the September spike, cancellation-during-billing has been growing steadily every month. This is the most concerning trend in the dataset and may indicate a worsening billing experience or a decline in perceived value.

Opportunities

  • Investigate and resolve the September 2025 bulk pause event — identify root cause and whether these customers can be re-engaged.
  • Introduce a 'soft pause' option with a minimum-commitment skip flow as the first offer when a customer contacts about cancelling.
  • Add exit-intent prompts in the cancellation flow asking for reasons, and automatically route high-LTV cancellers to a retention specialist.

6.3 · Meal Changes

Medium Priority · 6.7%

1,470 tickets · remarkably consistent month-to-month (5–8%)

Tickets are driven by customers requesting changes after billing closes, or being unsure of the meal selection process. Slight uptick in Q4 2025 and early 2026.

Key sub-themes: meal_changes_during_billing (1,263), how_to_change_meal_portion_aiz (1,216), meal_change_after_billing (173).

Customer Voice

Web"I want to change my order to the fan favorite for this week coming."
Email"I have updated my credit card details so payment should go through, but I am battling to access the meal kits to make my weekly choices."
Web"I need to choose my meals for next week but the website keeps showing last week's menu."

Trend Insight

The high volume of how_to_change_meal_portion_aiz tickets suggests customers are confused about where and how to change their meals — this is a UX discoverability problem, not just a timing issue.

Opportunities

  • In-app meal-selection reminders with a direct deep-link to the selection screen, sent 48 hours before billing cutoff.
  • Make the meal-change deadline more prominent on the dashboard (countdown timer or sticky banner).
  • Offer WhatsApp-based meal selection as a low-friction alternative to app navigation.

6.4 · Operations & Fulfilment

Medium Priority · 5.5%

1,192 tickets · December 2025 peak at 8.3%

Peaks in December 2025 and February 2026 (6.0%), consistent with festive season supply chain pressure. Most common issue: missing ingredients, followed by incorrect meals and damaged items.

Key sub-themes: missing_ingredient (425), incorrect_meal (99), missing_meal (70), damaged_ingredient (44), recipe_card_missing (161).

Customer Voice

Web"Hi there — I received 2 of the Beef Gochujang noodles instead of the Chicken Wings."
Email"Mexican Cottage Pie: Hi, there was no corn in the packet."
Email"I only received 3 boxes of 4 that I paid for. The courier also said he would escalate the missing box."

Trend Insight

The December 2025 spike is attributable to festive-season demand. The consistent baseline of ~5% suggests an ongoing operational accuracy gap. Missing recipe cards are a separate issue addressable with simple QA checklists.

Opportunities

  • Pre-dispatch packing checklist audit for high-risk SKUs (proteins, specialty ingredients).
  • For stock-out scenarios, notify customers before dispatch with a clear compensation offer rather than waiting for them to contact CS.
  • Add recipe-card verification to the packing-station workflow.

6.5 · Billing & Payment

Medium Priority · 2.9%

644 tickets · peak in October 2025 (5.1%)

Trended upward through Q2 and Q3 2025 (from 1.8% to 5.1%), then dipped in Q4 before rising again. Common issues: failed payments, refund requests, double charges, and difficulties updating card details.

Customer Voice

Email"It seems like last Wednesday's payment was taken twice on my account. Please see attached screenshot from my banking application."
Email"I am trying to upload my new bank card details but there seems to be a problem on your system. Can you please help me get this updated?"
Web"Hi — I did not order anything and you have billed me for 2 meals. All I did was sign up for future. Please cancel this order."

Trend Insight

The October 2025 peak (5.1%) coincides with the tail of the September subscription spike, suggesting billing failures may have been a trigger for the mass pause event. The double-billing complaint is a recurring theme that indicates a payment-processing edge case.

Opportunities

  • Send clear billing-confirmation emails showing exactly what was charged and why, with a direct link to the billing FAQ.
  • Build a self-service card-update flow that is reliably accessible from all devices and app versions.
  • Audit the September 2025 payment cycle for double-charge events.

6.6 · Product Quality

Medium Priority · 2.3%

492 tickets · seasonal decline through H2 2025, recovery in early 2026

Quality reports dropped from 3.2% in April to 1.4% in September and near 0% by December, before recovering in early 2026. Could reflect seasonal produce variability, supplier changes, or improved QA processes in H2 2025.

Key sub-themes: poor_quality_fruit_or_veg (133), poor_quality_chicken (60), quality (65), poor_quality_herbs_or_salad_leaves (46), poor_quality_beef (39).

Customer Voice

Web"Slimy lettuce with tonight's meal — lettuce has brown edges and is not fresh. I now have to go to the shop and buy more, which defeats the object of using UCOOK."
Email"The chicken breast that we received had gone off. It smelt completely bad and we could therefore not make the recipe."
Email"I am really concerned about the fresh produce we are receiving. The salads feel like they have little to no value as they are all wilted."

Trend Insight

The seasonal decline in quality complaints in H2 2025 may reflect winter produce being more stable, while the recovery in early 2026 suggests spring/summer freshness challenges returning. Fruit and vegetables are the most common category (133 reports), followed by chicken.

Opportunities

  • Implement a supplier quality scorecard with monthly review thresholds and escalation triggers.
  • Add a single-tap in-app quality-reporting feature that routes photos directly to the procurement team.
  • Provide seasonal sourcing updates to customers so they understand variability in produce.

6.7 · Onboarding & Account

Medium Priority · 3.0%

666 tickets · February 2026 spike to 8.6% (211 tickets)

Generally stable at 2–4%, but February 2026 saw an abnormal spike driven by sign-up assistance and password-reset requests (see §5.2). Outside this event, common issues include email mismatches, inability to complete sign-up, and confusion about delivery area coverage.

Customer Voice

Email"I wanted to order many times but I cannot confirm or log into my account. I've tried forgotten password but it asks to verify on my phone and when I type in my number it does not work."
Web"Can you please assign a competent agent to assist me. My name appears when I open your site but the email address is incorrect and CANNOT BE CHANGED."
Email"Do you by any chance deliver to other parts than Pretoria — for instance, Zinkwazi?"

Trend Insight

The February 2026 spike suggests a specific platform event. Outside of that, the steady baseline of sign-up friction indicates the onboarding flow has unresolved UX issues — particularly around phone verification and email management.

Opportunities

  • Streamline the OTP/phone verification flow — offer email verification as a fallback.
  • Allow customers to update their email address directly from account settings without requiring CS intervention.
  • Add a delivery-area checker at the top of the sign-up flow to prevent customers from completing registration in unserviced areas.

6.8 · Technical Issues

Low Priority · 1.3%

285 tickets · February 2026 spike to 7.8% (192 tickets)

The smallest pain-point cluster overall, but the February 2026 spike from a normal baseline of 0.1–0.8% indicates a significant platform incident. Issues include app-reactivation errors, GoNative app-update prompts, checkout failures, and login/password problems.

Customer Voice

Email"I am being told I need to reactivate my account but I am getting all sorts of errors when trying to do this."
Web"I have been locked out of my profile. When going into the app or on the web the message states I must re-activate my profile and choose a meal kit plan. When choosing a plan it just loops back."
Web"I can't add meals because I keep getting a popup saying I need to update my app from GoNative.io. I don't see any updates in the App Store."

Trend Insight

The February 2026 spike is almost certainly a single platform incident. The GoNative.io reference suggests the UCOOK app is built on a webview wrapper, and the forced-update prompt may have been triggered erroneously. The reactivation loop issue appears multiple times across the dataset as a persistent UX defect.

Opportunities

  • Prioritise fixing the reactivation-loop bug — it appears in tickets across multiple months and causes high customer frustration.
  • Review app-update prompts from GoNative to ensure they only trigger when an actual store update is available.
  • Set up automated alerting for technical contact spikes so engineering can respond within hours of a platform incident.

8. Methodology

Data Source

Zendesk ticket export covering 8 April 2025 – 11 March 2026. Total raw records: 26,101. After excluding Skoon-tagged tickets (4,242), the UCOOK dataset comprises 21,859 tickets.

Classification Approach

Tickets were classified using structured Zendesk tags applied by the UCOOK support team. Each ticket can carry multiple tags and was therefore eligible for classification into more than one cluster. Classification achieved 99.3% coverage, with the small unclassified remainder consisting of generic or incomplete tag sets.

Cluster Definitions

Eight operational clusters were derived from the dominant tag groups in the dataset. These were then cross-referenced against the five workshop pain-point clusters from Session 1 of the Customer Journey Workshop.

Trend Analysis

Monthly trend percentages represent each cluster's share of the total UCOOK ticket volume for that month. Raw counts are shown in brackets within trend tables. Spike detection uses a threshold of 1.8× the cluster's 12-month monthly average.

Customer Voice Excerpts

Verbatim quotes were drawn from the ticket Description field. Agent responses, email signatures, HTML tags, URLs, and quoted prior messages were stripped before sampling. Quotes have been selected to represent authentic, unedited customer language while avoiding personally identifiable information.
